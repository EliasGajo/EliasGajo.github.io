RIE with an O2 plasma recipe

An O2 plasma recipe is useful to isolate our very thin interesting regions for the rest of the MoS2 bulk. To do that, we will spin coat a PPA layer and pattern it such that the contours of the interesting regions are exposed to the air. Then, the O2 plasma will etch these contours and isolate our interesting regions. In this experiment we will not care about the etch rate in the MoS2 but only about the etch rate in the PPA. Indeed, the PPA etch rate will give us the maximum etching time (the time to etch all the PPA layer) and after we just need to ensure that this maximum etching time is enough to isolate our interesting regions.
Then, in the following experiments we want to determine approximately the etch rate of this plasma recipe in the PPA.

Process flow

The following process flow shows how we planned to measure the etched PPA thickness after a RIE run.

Step 1 : Dice a chip of Si + SiO2 and spin coat a PPA layer on it.

Step 2 : Pattern the PPA such that the SiO2 is exposed to the air. Mask a portion of the PPA layer with something (Here another chip).

Step 3 : Run the reactive ion etching during a time such that not all the PPA is etched.

Step 4 : Remove the mask from the PPA layer and measure the pattern depth and the height of the step induced by the mask (with an AFM).

Substrate preparation

  • Dice a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2 into small chips (2cm x 2cm)
  • Clean the chip in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Clean the chip in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dry the chip with a N2 gun
  • Spin coat some PPA 3% at 2000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 60 nm)

Reactive ion etching

For this RIE experiment, we want to investigate the recipe called "O2 Clean_withWafer_NoEPD" in Cmi. That is an O2 recipe with 100 sccm flow rates. The total pressure is 20 mTorr.

Chip preparation

To mask a part of the PPA (as explained in the process flow, step 2), we diced a small part of our Si + SiO2 wafer and we stick it on our chip with Kapton tape.

RIE run

We run a RIE session with the "O2 Clean_withWafer_NoEPD" recipe during 2 seconds.

PPA step measurement

The AFM (NanoFrazor's AFM) imaging of the PPA step is too bad (the region is too noisy) and we cannot determine anything.
Then we determine the step height with a reflectometer. The reflectometer measurement of the PPA thickness was around 60 nm in the masked region and 14 nm in the exposed one. Hence the step height should correspond to something like 46 nm.
