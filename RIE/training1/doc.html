RIE with a He/H2/C4F8 recipe

This plasma recipe is interesting for the pattern transfer of the PPA in the SiO2. Then, in the following experiments we want to determine the etch rate of this plasma recipe in the PPA and in the SiO2. We just want to have a good approximation of these quantities so we perform only fast and easy experiments.

Process flow

The following process flow shows how we planned to measure the etched PPA thickness and the etched SiO2 thickness after a RIE run.

Step 1 : Dice a chip of Si + SiO2 and spin coat a PPA layer on it.

Step 2 : Pattern the PPA such that the SiO2 is exposed to the air. Mask a portion of the PPA layer with something (Here another chip).

Step 3 : Run the reactive ion etching during a time such that not all the PPA is etched.

Step 4 : Remove the mask from the PPA layer and measure the height of the step induced by the mask (with an AFM).

Step 5 : Remove the PPA layer with an oxygen plasma and measure the depth of the SiO2 patterns (with an AFM).

Substrate preparation

  • Diced part of a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2
  • Cleaned in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Cleaned in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dried with a N2 gun
