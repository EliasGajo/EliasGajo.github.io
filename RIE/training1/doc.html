Experiment 1

He/H2/C4F8 plasma recipes are interesting for the pattern transfer of the PPA in the SiO2. There are 2 recipes containing these gases in CMi (for the SPTS APS) : "SiO2 PR 3:1" and "SiO2 PR 5:1" which differ in the flow rates.
In this experiment, we focus on the "SiO2 PR 3:1" and we want to determine its etch rate in PPA and in SiO2. We just want to have good approximations of these quantities so we perform only fast and easy experiments.

Process flow

The following process flow shows how we planned to measure the etched PPA thickness and the etched SiO2 thickness after a RIE run.

Step 1 : Dice a chip of Si + SiO2 and spin coat a PPA layer on it.

Step 2 : Pattern the PPA such that the SiO2 is exposed to the air. Mask a portion of the PPA layer with something (Here another chip).

Step 3 : Run the reactive ion etching during a time such that not all the PPA is etched.

Step 4 : Remove the mask from the PPA layer and measure the height of the step induced by the mask (with an AFM).

Step 5 : Remove the PPA layer with an oxygen plasma and measure the depth of the SiO2 patterns (with an AFM).

Substrate preparation

  • Dice a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2 into small chips (2cm x 2cm)
  • Clean the chip in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Clean the chip in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dry the chip with a N2 gun
  • Spin coat some PPA 3% at 2000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 60 nm)

PPA patterning

We need to pattern some shapes such that they locally remove all the PPA thickness. The theoretical thickness of the PPA is 60 nm, but we choose a target depth of 70 nm to have some margin (In any cases the SiO2 cannot be etched by t-SPL).

Reactive ion etching

For this RIE experiment, we want to investigate the recipe called "SiO2 PR 3:1" in Cmi. That is a He/H2/C4F8 recipe with 175/18/15 sccm flow rates. The total pressure is 4 mTorr.

Chip preparation

To mask a part of the PPA (as explained in the process flow, step 2), we diced a small part of our Si + SiO2 wafer and we stick it on our chip with Kapton tape.

RIE run

We run a RIE session with the "SiO2 PR 3:1" recipe during 30 seconds.

PPA measurement

This part is not as simple as it seems. The main problem is the step is not clean and we have not a sharp transition between the masked region and the unmasked one. And as we work with the NanoFrazor's AFM, we can't scan a region bigger than 57x57 um2. So here are the brut results of two AFM captures on the transition region.

Scan 1

Scan 2

It is difficult to read anything from these plots as there is high frequencies oscillations and the slope of the substrate hasn't been removed. Let's improve that by correcting the slope and implementing a low pass filter.

Scan 1 with filter

Scan 2 with filter

These low pass filters are moving average filters with a radius of around 4.4 um. With that, we can read the PPA step height which is approximately 20 nm and 16 nm (respectively).
But as the step region is very noisy, the AFM measure is not the best solution. Then we also determine the step height with a reflectometer. The reflectometer measurement of the PPA thickness was around 60 nm in the masked region and 30 nm in the exposed one. Hence the step height should correspond to something like 30 nm. The corresponding PPA etch rate should be around 60 nm / min.
