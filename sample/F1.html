Final gas sensors fabrication

The fabrication consists of 3 main steps :

  • Pattern the desired surface topography in the SiO2.
  • Transfer the MoS2 and isolate the active region.
  • Deposit the electrodes and do the wire bonding.

Sensor 1

This gas sensor has a wavy surface topography. Here are the fabrication steps :

Wavy surface patterning

Introduction

We want the SiO2 to have a wavy surface with a good aspect ratio. To do that, we applied the following steps :

  • Spin coat a PPA layer
  • Pattern wavy surfaces in the PPA
  • Transfer and increase these patterns into the SiO2
These steps have been studied and we kept the best result from pattern transfer, Experiment 3.

Substrate preparation

  • Diced part of a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2
  • Cleaned in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Cleaned in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dried with a N2 gun

PPA spin coating

Role of this layer : Layer to pattern with t-SPL technique.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Spin coated PPA 3% at 2000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 60 nm)
  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 60 nm
Thickness measured : No

Reactive ion etching

To transfer the patterns into the SiO2 layer, we use reactive ion etching with a He/H2/C4F8 plasma solution (the one called SiO2 PR 3:1 in CMi) during 20 seconds ( the optimal time is extracted from the RIE experiments).

MoS2 transfer and isolation

Introduction

We want now to deposit the MoS2 on our wavy surface and to .

  • MoS2 flake exfoliation and transfer
  • PMGI spin coating
  • PPA spin coating
  • Pattern the contours of the active region in the PPA
  • Open the PMGI under the patterned contours by wet etching
  • Cut the contours by RIE and remove the resists by lift-off

PMGI spin coating

Role of this layer : Sacrificial layer for the lift-off.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 5 minutes (for dehydration)
  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes

  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes

Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Active region isolation

Here are the steps followed for the active region isolation :

  • Remove the PPA on the contours of our active region with the NanoFrazor.
  • Wet etching into a TMAH solution (AZ 726 MIF) during 260 seconds.
  • RIE with a CHF3/SF6 plasma recipe (Si3N4_Smooth) during 5 seconds.

Contours patterning

After RIE + lift-off

Remark

The surface where we transferred the MoS2 became hydrophobic and it was difficult to spin coat on it. It was still possible to spin coat but the PMGI layer on the active region was smaller than on the other parts. The wet etching time was hence to big and almost all the PMGI is gone on the active region. That's why we used the CHF3/SF6 plasma recipe rather than an oxygen plasma for the active region isolation (as an oxygen plasma require a high thickness of resist as shown in Active region isolation, Experiment 1).

Electrodes deposition

Introduction

As we want to fabricate a chemiresistor, we only need two electrodes and to contact them onto the edges of the isolated active region.

Illustration of the objective

Then, the main steps to do this are the following :

  • Spin coat a PMGI layer
  • Spin coat a PPA layer
  • Pattern the electrodes as well as the contacts into the PPA
  • Wet etching such that we open the PMGI under the patterned zones of the PPA
  • Metals evaporation and lift-off
