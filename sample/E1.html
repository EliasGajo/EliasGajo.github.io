E1

This chip contains some exfoliated flakes of MoS2. These flakes have been transferred onto a Si + SiO2 substrate without any patterns. Then the aim of this sample (as well as E2, E3, and E4) is to define and isolate the active regions of our 2D material. We will also use the successful active regions to fabricate a plane surface gas sensor.

Hydrophilic treatment

The surface where the MoS2 has been transferred became hydrophobe (due to the scotch tape) and doesn't allow us to spin coat a PPA layer on it. Then, we apply the following treatment in order to remove this hydrophobicity.

  • Chip deeped in a remover 1165 solution during 30 minutes
  • Chip deeped in a AZ726MIF solution during 30 minutes
  • Chip rinsed in water
  • Chip rinsed in IPA
  • Chip baked at 110°C during 3 minutes for dehydration

PMGI spin coating

The PPA layer cannot be too thick (as we cannot pattern too deep shapes with the NanoFrazor), hence we add a PMGI layer to protect the interesting regions during the RIE. Furthermore, the surface of the PPA layer will be more flat with a PMGI layer underneath and the patterning will be easier and better.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 5 minutes (for dehydration)
  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes
  • Let it cool down
  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 180 nm
Thickness measured : No

PPA spin coating

Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Spin coated PPA 3% at 6000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 32 nm)
  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 32 nm
Thickness measured : No

RIE

Now we run the reactive ion etching with the "O2 Clean_withWafer_NoEPD" recipe during 10 seconds. This recipe was experimented in RIE training 3 and RIE training 4 and has a high etch rate in the PPA unlike the MoS2. But now that we also have a PMGI layer (of around 180 nm), our chip resists a longer etching time (in this case 10 seconds).

Region 4 after RIE

Region 5 after RIE

Now, it seems that our interesting regions are partially isolated (as we notice a very thin layer under the contours). Then, we can only use this procedure to isolate interesting regions if we are sure that these regions are very thin (probably 2 - 3 atomic layers maximum).
